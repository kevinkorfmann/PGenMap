Mapping the evolution of population genetics

A data-driven map of the global population-genetics research community — who works where, on what, with whom, and above all how the field's questions and methods have shifted across four decades.

The four time axes

  • Topic evolution — embedding-based themes tracked year by year: classical coalescent → the ancient-DNA revolution → the machine-learning turn → the tree-sequence era.
  • Method trajectories — adoption curves for specific tools: coalescent, ABC, PSMC/MSMC, ancient DNA, SLiM, msprime/tskit, deep learning.
  • Collaboration networks — co-authorship communities — the "schools" — and how they form, merge, and bridge across decades.
  • Careers & citation flow — how individual labs shift focus over a career, and which ideas became influential when.
